What is taekwondo?

Taekwondo is a Korean martial art with a literal translation of "kicking" (tae), "punching" (kwon), and "the art or way of" (do). It emphasizes head-height kicks, jumping and spinning kicks, and fast kicking techniques. The goal is to give a sense of self-esteem, knowledge of self-defense, heightened mental and physical well being.

You must meet the following requirements to be considered an UAlberta student:
  • Currently enrolled as a student at the University of Alberta
  • At least 17 years old
Our club aims to provide the most accessible way for students to practice martial arts. Our fees are extremely cheap and consists of:
  • The club fee goes directly into the club to pay for room rentals, events, new equipment, and cleaning supplies.
  • The Athlete Information Management System (AIMs) fee goes to fund the university's Club Sports program.
  • The Van Vliet Complex (VVC) membership provides access to the university's fitness facilities and is already paid for in your non-instructional fees.
Pay the club fee by e-transfering $20 to the club's email, crtkd@ualberta.ca:
  • Login to your online banking.
  • Click on Send an Interac e-Transfer.
  • Add a new Interac e-Transfer Recipient and enter $20 for amount, then hit Next.
  • Enter UAlberta Taekwondo Club for name and crtkd@ualberta.ca for email, then hit Next.
  • Review the details, and then hit Send. Auto-deposit is set up, so no security question is necessary.
Pay the $10 AIMs fee:
  • Go to ualberta.athletesystems.ca.
  • Click on Create your own account, if you don't already have one. This is NOT your CCID login!
  • Sign in to AIMS.
  • Click on Register for a team or club.
  • Select Taekwondo Club from the dropdown menu and then click on Begin registration.
  • Fill in your personal information.
  • Thoroughly read through the legal documents, and select Yes - I agree from the dropdown once read.
  • Click on Checkout, and then fill in your card details to pay the $10 fee.

Frequently Asked Questions

I don't have any taekwondo experience, can I still join?
Yes! We have lots of beginners in the club.
Practices have already started, can I still join?
Yes! We are constantly taking in new members. You can try out your first practice for free before making a commitment.
Is there belt testing?
Unfortunately, the club does not provide belt testing.
How many practices do I have to attend each week?
The club is very flexible and there is no required number of practices to attend; you can come as much or as little as you want!
What do I need to bring to practice?
A change of clothes, a water bottle, and yourself. Most members wear a t-shirt and pants/shorts.
How long are practices and do I need to stay the entire time?
Our practices are generally between 1 and 2 hours long. But don't worry if you can only join for part of the practice! Just make sure to warm up and stretch on your own time.
Where will practices be held?
Practices are held in the Education building, room GB-23. You can find the room in the basement at the end of the hallway.
Do I need to wear dobok (taekwondo training uniform) to practice?
No, dobok is not required. Many of our members opt for t-shirt and shorts. If you have a dobok, you are more than welcome to wear it to practice!
Are practices separated into skill levels, so that all participants are improving?
While practice times are not segregated by level, the instructors will adjust drills to better fit the levels of different people. For example when we do kicking drills, advanced people will be given longer and more challenging combos and during fitness portions, they will be given more reps/sets. We also do our best to group people together who have similar experience levels. This way everyone can enjoy the feeling of improving together!
Do I need to purchase any equipment?
No, all equipment is provided by the club for each practice.
Can non-students join the club?
Yes, we accept non-students, including alumni, staff, and students not attending UAlberta. However, non-students must be 18 years or older.
Who instructs the practices?
We are a student-led club and our instructors are volunteer students. All of our instructors are black belts and have years of martial arts experience. Some even teach outside of the club.
How much are club fees?
Club fees are $30 per semester for students:
  1. $20 to be paid via e-transfer to the club email crtkd@ualberta.ca.
  2. $10 to Campus Rec via the AIMs website.
All members must also have a valid VVC membership.
Go to fees for detailed instructions on how to pay.
